Who was Bernie Mac?

Bernie Mac was a stand-up comedian, actor and voice actor before his death August 9, 2008 at 50 years old. Born and raised in Chicago, Illinois, Bernie made a living on bringing joy to others with his hilarious comedy. He's mostly remembered for his role in "The Original Kings of Comedy ", "Ocean's Eleven" and his very own sitcom: "The Bernie Mac Show". He leaves behind his both his wife, Rhonda McCullough and daughter Je'neice Childress.

Milestones and Moments:

  • 1957 - October 5th, Benard "Bernie Mac" McCullough was born in Chicago, Illinois.
  • 1973 - At 16 years old, Bernie loses his mother, Mary McCullough to cancer.
  • 1973 - Month after the passing of his mother, Bernie relocates to Tampa, Florida.
  • 1975 - After moving back to Chicago, Bernie graduates from Chicago Vocational High School.
  • 1977 - Bernie marries his high school sweetheart Rhonda Gore in September.
  • 1978 - Rhonda gives birth to Bernie's first and only child: Ja'Neice McCollough.
  • 1989 - Bernie wins the Miller Lite Comedy Search at 32, jumpstarting his stand-up career.
  • 1992 - Bernie's had first cameo in the comedy crime film: Mo' Money and performed on HBO's Def Comedy Jam transitionin him into acting.
  • 1995 - Bernie got his big break in the film Friday, as "Pastor Clever"

    The popular television network Fox, gave Bernie his own sitcom in 2001, called The Bernie Mac show. Where Bernie was able to protray a fictional version of himself but still include parodies of his actual life and stay true to his stand-up comedy roots. The show actually allowed him to be nominated for 2 Emmy Awards, 2 Golden Globe Awards, and winning 4 NAACP Image Awards, a PRISM Award and 2 Satellite Awards before his passing in August of 2008.
